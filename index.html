Bank Comparison for Newly Incorporated Private Limited Company

Prepared for: Management Review  |  Date: 26 March 2026  |  Country: India

Est. Annual Turnover: ₹10 Cr+ Entity Type: Pvt. Ltd. (transitioning from Partnership Firm) Women-Founded (>51% ownership) Focus: Current A/c · OD · Women Schemes
🏆

Top Pick — Private Bank

ICICI Bank

  • 1Handles ₹10 Cr+ annual transactions smoothly — unlimited free NEFT/RTGS/IMPS and ₹5 L free cash deposit per month via iBizz portal
  • 2Zero-balance Startup Account for 6 months + fee waivers upon DPIIT recognition — ideal for a newly incorporated Pvt. Ltd.
  • 3OD / working capital limit available after 6–12 months; typically 20–25% of turnover (₹2–2.5 Cr for ₹10 Cr revenue)
  • 4Women-founder benefits via ICICI Startup Banking page — concessional processing fees and priority credit appraisal
  • 5Best-in-class digital app (iBizz), GST/TDS integration, maker-checker access, and dedicated RM for business accounts
🏆

Top Pick — Government Bank

State Bank of India

  • 1Highest free cash deposit limit (₹6 L/month) and lowest minimum balance — best fit for ₹10 Cr+ annual transaction volumes
  • 2Stree Shakti Package — dedicated interest rate concession for women-owned businesses (>50% shareholding by women founders)
  • 3Mandatory Stand-Up India nodal officer at every branch — women founders can access composite loans up to ₹1 Cr without extensive collateral
  • 4Partnership firm's existing SBI history (if any) directly accelerates OD approval for the new Pvt. Ltd. — strongest legacy migration path
  • 5CGTMSE-backed OD up to ₹2 Cr without third-party guarantee — critical advantage for a company in its first year
Important: Figures below are based on publicly available Schedule of Charges (SOC), product leaflets, and official government portals as of early 2026. Rates, MAB, and limits change periodically. Always verify the exact current figures directly with the bank's relationship manager or their official SOC PDF before signing any account-opening form. 📄 Overdraft (OD) / Cash Credit (CC) Facility — What to Expect
Parameter Typical Norm Notes / How Banks Decide Source
When will banks offer OD to a new Pvt. Ltd.? After 6–12 months of account operation with demonstrable turnover New company has no P&L history. Banks first watch cash-flow pattern in the current account. Some PSBs may insist on 1 full audited year. RBI MSME Guidelines
OD Limit as % of Revenue (Turnover-Based) 20% – 25% of projected / actual annual turnover For ₹10 Cr turnover → expect OD limit of ₹2–2.5 Cr (working capital). This is a general industry norm; exact % depends on sector, collateral, credit score. RBI FAQ – Working Capital
OD Against Collateral (Property / FD) Up to 60–75% of collateral value OD against Fixed Deposit: up to 90% of FD value. Against commercial property: 60–65% of value. This is the fastest route for a new company. SBI OD Product
Clean / Unsecured OD (no collateral) Generally not offered to companies < 2 years old Requires minimum 2 years of audited financials and good CIBIL/CRIF score. Exception: fintech NBFCs (Razorpay Capital, Lendingkart) may offer ₹5–50L unsecured. SIDBI Products
Interest Rate on OD / CC Repo Rate + 2.5% to 5% spread (currently ~11%–14% p.a.) RBI Repo rate as of 2026: ~6.25%. Bank MCLR adds 2–3% spread. Women-founder concessional schemes can reduce effective rate by 0.25–0.5%. RBI Repo Rate
GST Registration requirement Mandatory All banks require GST certificate, MOA/AOA, Board Resolution, and 6-month bank statement (from earlier partnership firm) for OD application. GST Portal
Govt.-Backed OD (CGTMSE Cover) Up to ₹2 Cr without collateral (CGTMSE guaranteed) Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises covers loans/OD up to ₹2 Cr without third-party guarantee — very useful for a new Pvt. Ltd. CGTMSE Portal
Practical Tip for Your Company: Since you are transitioning from a Partnership Firm, bring along the last 2–3 years of partnership firm bank statements and ITR when meeting the bank RM. Banks will use this as a proxy for business vintage and may fast-track OD approval, even for the new Pvt. Ltd. entity.
♀ Special Benefits for Women Founders / Women-Led Companies

Stand-Up India Scheme All Scheduled Banks

  • Loans from ₹10 Lakh to ₹1 Crore for women entrepreneurs (or SC/ST)
  • Covers greenfield projects (first-time ventures in manufacturing, services, or trade)
  • Composite loan including term loan + working capital
  • Repayment up to 7 years; moratorium up to 18 months
  • Mandatory for all scheduled commercial banks (RBI mandate)
  • Apply via: standupmitra.in

SBI Stree Shakti Package SBI

  • For enterprises where women hold >50% ownership
  • Concession of 0.05% on interest rate for loans above ₹2 Lakh
  • Linked to Entrepreneurship Development Programme (EDP) training
  • Available for trade, small industries, and professional services
  • Source: SBI Stree Shakti

MUDRA Yojana – Mahila Udyam Nidhi PSBs / MFIs

  • Shishu: Up to ₹50,000 | Kishor: ₹50,001–₹5 L | Tarun: ₹5L–₹10L
  • No collateral required
  • Women-led businesses given priority disbursement
  • Applies to non-farm micro/small enterprises
  • Source: mudra.org.in

Bank of Baroda – Baroda Mahila Shakti Bank of Baroda

  • Specially designed loan product for women-owned MSMEs
  • Concessional interest rates with CGTMSE cover
  • Flexible repayment structures
  • Zero processing fees for select schemes
  • Source: Bank of Baroda MSME

SIDBI Mahila Udyam Nidhi Scheme SIDBI

  • For small-scale industries where women hold >51% equity
  • Soft loans up to ₹25 Lakh per project
  • Repayment period: 10 years (including 5 years moratorium)
  • Administered through State Financial Corporations and scheduled banks
  • Source: sidbi.in

DPIIT Startup India Recognition All Banks

  • Once DPIIT-recognised, ICICI, HDFC, Axis, Kotak offer 0-balance accounts and fee waivers
  • Access to self-certification compliance (environment, labour laws)
  • Tax exemption under Section 80-IAC (3 years)
  • Unlocks bank-specific startup credit programs
  • Source: Startup India Portal

WE Hub / State-Level Women Schemes State Govts.

  • Many state govts (Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Karnataka, Maharashtra) have specific women entrepreneur grants / subsidies
  • Tamil Nadu: TANSIM / WE Hub for women-led startups
  • Often provides interest subsidy of 3–5% on eligible loans
  • Check with your state's Industries Department
  • Source: MSME Ministry – Women

Interest Rate Concession Summary

  • SBI: 0.05% concession (Stree Shakti)
  • Stand-Up India: Concessional composite rate; no specific % mandated — negotiate with bank
  • CGTMSE-backed: Effectively saves 1–2% as no collateral is needed
  • MUDRA: Subsidised rates through refinancing to banks
  • Tip: Mention women-founder status explicitly when negotiating OD rate — banks have internal guidelines to incentivise this
✍ Recommended Next Steps
  1. Get DPIIT Startup Recognition first — it unlocks 0-balance accounts and fee waivers at ICICI, HDFC, and Axis before you even open an account. Apply at startupindia.gov.in.
  2. Shortlist 2 banks (1 private + 1 government) — e.g., ICICI (private) + SBI (government) — and request a Relationship Manager meeting at each with your company incorporation documents.
  3. Carry to the RM meeting: Certificate of Incorporation, MOA/AOA, PAN, GST certificate, last 2–3 years partnership firm bank statements + ITR, and Director ID proofs.
  4. Specifically ask the RM about: (a) women-founder concession, (b) current OD eligibility based on past partnership firm turnover, (c) CGTMSE coverage on OD, (d) processing fee waivers under any scheme.
  5. Verify Schedules of Charges (SOC): Search "[Bank Name] Current Account Schedule of Charges 2026 PDF" on Google to get the legally binding fee list before signing.
  6. Check Stand-Up India eligibility at standupmitra.in — this is a Central Government mandate and every scheduled bank must have a dedicated nodal officer for this.
